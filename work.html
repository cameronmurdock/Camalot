What I
Actually Do

Systems, platforms, and structures for collective action

I design systems that people rely on, not abstractions that look good in decks.

My work spans platform design, data modeling, operations, and economic structure — always grounded in real environments.

Platform & Systems Design

I design end-to-end systems that support:

  • coordination across teams and groups,
  • clear roles and responsibilities,
  • task tracking tied to real outcomes,
  • and long-term maintainability.

This includes product architecture, UX flows, and system boundaries.

Ontology & Data Modeling

I develop graph-based data models that reflect how communities actually function.

Instead of flattening everything into "posts" or "users," I model:

  • people, groups, events, tasks, roles, resources, and flows,
  • their relationships,
  • and the permissions that govern interaction.

This enables interoperability without loss of meaning — across platforms, organizations, and time.

Regenerative Economic Design

I work on economic structures that avoid extraction by default.

This includes:

  • surplus distribution models,
  • shared treasuries,
  • cooperative and commons-based finance,
  • contribution-aware value flows,
  • and non-speculative incentive systems.
The goal is not growth at all costs —
it's viability with integrity.

AI for Operations (Not Hype)

I design AI systems to:

  • reduce administrative burden,
  • automate repetitive coordination tasks,
  • surface insight without surveillance,
  • and support decision-making without replacing judgment.

AI should make systems simpler to live inside, not harder to understand.
