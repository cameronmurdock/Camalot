Ideas that shape the work

Most modern crises are coordination failures, not moral ones.

This page outlines the ideas that shape my work.

Social Infrastructure vs Social Media

Social media optimizes for attention.
Social infrastructure optimizes for function.

Communities don't need more visibility — they need better ways to organize labor, care, resources, and time.

Coordination Is the Bottleneck

We already have enough talent, technology, and goodwill.

What we lack are systems that:

  • make participation legible,
  • align incentives without coercion,
  • and scale trust without flattening relationships.

Technology Should Respect Place

Most platforms erase locality.
I design for place-aware systems — geographic, cultural, and ecological.

Local context is not noise.
It's the signal.

Repair Over Disruption

I'm not interested in "moving fast and breaking things."

I'm interested in:

  • repairing institutions,
  • restoring capacity,
  • and building systems that can be stewarded across generations.
