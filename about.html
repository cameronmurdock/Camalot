About
Cameron

Background and philosophy

I've spent my life studying how people organize themselves — culturally, economically, and technically.

My background spans:

  • community organizing and venue operations,
  • systems and ontology design,
  • cooperative governance and finance,
  • product development grounded in lived environments.

Personal Philosophy

I believe:

  • systems shape behavior,
  • incentives matter more than intentions,
  • and good infrastructure is mostly invisible.
I build for the long term, for real use, and for people who live with the consequences.

Background

Before focusing on systems design, I worked across different domains — from grassroots organizing to cooperative business development to technical product work.

This interdisciplinary background shapes how I approach problems:

  • I start with real use cases, not abstractions
  • I design for maintainability and stewardship
  • I center the people who will live with the systems I build

Current Focus

Today, I'm focused on Rivr.Social and related work on coordination infrastructure — building tools that help communities organize effectively without losing their autonomy or soul.

I'm based in Boulder, Colorado, but work with people and organizations across the world who are serious about building alternatives to extractive platforms.
